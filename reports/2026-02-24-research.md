### AI-Directed Large Codebase Migration as a Service
- **Source:** https://ladybird.org/posts/adopting-rust/ (1,147 points, 630 comments on HN)
- **Idea:** Andreas Kling ported Ladybird's entire JavaScript engine (25,000 lines) from C++ to Rust in two weeks using Claude Code and Codex. The methodology: (1) human decides what to port and in what order, (2) hundreds of small prompts steering the agent, (3) byte-for-byte identical output requirement between old and new pipelines, (4) adversarial review passes with different models, (5) extensive conformance testing (test262 + 12,461 regression tests). Zero regressions. The HN discussion crystallizes the key principle: "Don't improve during the port ‚Äî translate first, then make idiomatic later."
- **Why now:** Every company with a legacy codebase is thinking about modernization. This is the first high-profile case where the methodology is clearly documented, repeatable, and produced verifiable results at scale. The 10x speed improvement (2 weeks vs. months) makes the business case trivial.
- **Effort to test:** Medium ‚Äî Brian could offer "AI-assisted codebase migration" as a consulting service, using the Ladybird methodology as the proof point. Start with smaller projects (library ports, framework upgrades) to build a portfolio.
