### Taalas HC1 ‚Äî Model-in-Silicon at 17K Tokens/Sec
- **Source:** https://taalas.com/the-path-to-ubiquitous-ai/ + https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47086181 (719 points, 407 comments)
- **What it does:** Canadian startup Taalas hardwires AI models directly into silicon ‚Äî merging storage and compute on a single chip at DRAM-level density. Their first product runs Llama 3.1 8B at 17,000 tokens/sec (10x state of the art), 20x cheaper to produce, 10x less energy per token. No HBM, no liquid cooling, no advanced packaging. They can turn around a new model-in-silicon in ~2 months. $200M VC raised, founders from AMD/Nvidia.
- **How we'd use it:** This is early-stage but signals where inference is heading: dedicated silicon for specific models at extreme speed/cost ratios. When this scales to larger models, it could enable always-on AI features at negligible cost. For now, their API could be useful for latency-sensitive applications (voice agents, real-time processing). Try their demo at chatjimmy.ai.

### Cord ‚Äî Tree-Based Multi-Agent Coordination
- **Source:** https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47096466 (69 points, 37 comments)
- **What it does:** A research framework for coordinating trees of AI agents using spawn/fork primitives. Key HN discussion insight: ~40% of tokens in multi-agent systems go to coordination overhead (status checking, conflict resolution, duplicate work detection). The proposed solution: "claim-before-act" ‚Äî agents announce what they're about to work on before starting, so others avoid duplication. Also proposed: "context query" as a first-class primitive for compacting context passed between agents.
- **How we'd use it:** Directly relevant to OpenClaw's sub-agent architecture. The "claim-before-act" pattern could reduce wasted tokens in parallel research/coding tasks. The context query concept (natural language instructions for how to compact context) is worth experimenting with in AGENTS.md or sub-agent task prompts.

### Nutrient ‚Äî From SDK to 8-Figure ARR via Acqui-Hire
- **Source:** https://www.indiehackers.com/post/tech/building-a-mobile-sdk-and-expanding-it-into-an-8-figure-arr-portfolio-8OKoH1866puep8tLh3cr
- **What it does:** Jonathan Rhyne built PSPDFKit (PDF viewing/annotation SDK for mobile), bootstrapped it for 7 years, got acquired by Insight Partners in 2021, then quadrupled ARR to high tens of millions. Three business lines: SDKs, marketplace integrations (SharePoint/Salesforce), and direct-to-enterprise.
- **How we'd use it:** The pattern ‚Äî build an SDK solving one specific problem, bootstrap with early community traction, expand into adjacent use cases, then take strategic investment to accelerate ‚Äî is a proven playbook for any developer tool. If Brian builds marine services tooling, the SDK-first model (build the core engine, let others integrate it) could be the right architecture.
